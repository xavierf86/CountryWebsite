Chile: Culture

Chile History Traditions Culture Landmarks

Family and Values

Chileans are known for having strong family values. Most families are very close, and it is common for relatives to live near each other or visit every week. Having respect for parents and elders is extremely important, and this is always passed down through the familys. Celebrations like birthdays and holidays are usually spent with large gatherings, food, and music. Family is very important for Chileans, as it is apart of their daily lifes.

Music and Dances

Chilean music mixes indigenous, Spanish, and modern music. The most famous traditional dance is the Cueca, which uses handkerchiefs and guitar music. The most well known, Folk music, is important in many regions, especially in the countryside. Today, Chile also has popular genres like rock, cumbia, and other latin music. Music and dance are used in festivals, celebrations, and cultural events across the whole country.

Sports and Soccer

Soccer is the most popular sport in Chile. The national team, known as “La Roja”, is loved across the country, and has won major championships, like the Copa America, most noticabley back in 2015 and 2016, where they won back to back. Chileans are passionate fans, and game days bring people together in homes, parks, and at stadiums. Other sports like rodeo, tennis, and basketball are also important, but soccer is the biggest sport in the country.

Chilean Food

Chilean food comes from a mix of native and Spanish influence. The most common foods are empanadas with meat, pastel de choclo, which is a sweet corn pie, and completos, which are Chilean styled hot dogs with tomato, avocado, and mayo. Foods can be different by region, but they all show Chile's connection to family meals. Food also plays an important role in family gatherings and cultural events.
